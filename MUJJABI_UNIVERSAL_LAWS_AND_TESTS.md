# Mujjabi Universal Laws and Tests

This file names the Part IV translations used across the manuscript archive.
The names are release vocabulary; their empirical status remains open until
independently tested.

## Universal Capacity Law

When sustained flow `Phi` approaches or exceeds effective system capacity,
constraint `C` rises and throughput becomes nonlinear. In Part IV this maps to
grid congestion, ecological pressure, market stress, inflammatory burden,
plasma instability, and information overload.

Testable form: increasing input stops improving output once the dominant
constraint proxy rises faster than recovery.

## Universal Memory Law

Systems can retain prior load in `M_s`: ecological degradation, infrastructure
fatigue, institutional backlog, immune priming, hysteresis in physical systems,
or entrenched cognitive priors. A short stress can therefore leave a long-lived
constraint state.

Testable form: after equal current load, systems with greater prior stress
history shows slower recovery or a higher residual constraint state.

## Cross-Domain Isomorphism Test

Two domains may share the same CDFD form without sharing the same material
mechanism. The claim is not that a city, immune system, and plasma are identical;
it is that they can be compared when the variables and scales are explicitly
mapped.

Testable form: matched flow, constraint, responsiveness, and memory proxies
show similar overload or recovery curves under standardized perturbation.

## Constraint-Relaxation Test

Recovery depends on `beta`, the effective relaxation rate of constraint. A
system with high flow and slow relaxation enters overload earlier than a
system with the same flow and faster relaxation.

Testable form: increasing recovery capacity lowers peak `Psi_s` and
shorten memory persistence after a perturbation.

## Adapter Stress Test

The runtime domain adapter sweep is a model-level test. It asks whether the
current CDFD Runtime can map selected domains through a common field update and
produce finite, interpretable outputs.

Testable form: adapters return finite outputs, explicit regimes, and
stable JSON artifacts. Non-finite outputs or uninterpretable regimes indicate a
runtime or modelling failure, not a discovery.

## Universal Cascade Test

A localized high-drive hub with slow constraint relaxation creates a
network stress response, including overload and memory locking, in the release
diagnostic.

Testable form: rerunning `Part_E_Synthesis/supplementary/run_partiv_discovery.py`
produces a finite cascade record under declared parameters. Changing the
drive, threshold, relaxation, or topology shifts the cascade boundary in
a predictable direction.
